Ranthambhore Tiger Reserve, located in the desert state of Rajasthan, Western India is renowned as a tiger heaven and holds the status of India's premier and most iconic tiger reserve. It boasts a healthy population of these majestic cats, offering excellent opportunities for sightings. It is a dry deciduous forest with an arid habitat, receiving the least amount of rainfall among all tiger reserves in India. This results in fewer patches of tall grass, along with an excellent network of forest tracks, contributing to exceptional tiger sightings.

Pros : Tiger sightings at their best thanks to a healthy population of big cats and great visibility.

Cons : Due to its popularity and central location in Rajasthan, it can become crowded during peak tourist seasons.

However If you can look past the sometimes crowded sightings you will find an experience in Ranthambhore unlike any other. It is the land of tigers and is an intoxicating mix of nature, history and wildlife.

Bandhavgarh Tiger Reserve

Bandhavgarh Tiger Reserve, situated in the central Indian state of Madhya Pradesh is renowned for its high density of tigers and ranks among the best places to see tigers in the wild. It is is often referred to as "The Temple of the Tiger" by veterans and "The Tiger Paradise" by photographers. The landscape of Bandhavgarh is captivating, with its sal forests, rolling hills, and ancient fort perched atop a hill. The picturesque surroundings provide a stunning backdrop for wildlife photography. Bandhavgarh is also known for its passionate guides and drivers who exhibit unparalleled dedication to tracking tigers.

Pros : Bandhavgarh Tiger Reserve offers an exceptional opportunity to witness tigers in their natural habitat and enjoy the scenic beauty of the region.

Cons: Bandhavgarh's popularity among wildlife enthusiasts and photographers means that the park can become crowded, especially during peak seasons. Also its not as easily accessible as Ranthambhore due to its remote location.

MOST BEAUTIFUL PLACES TO SEE TIGERS IN THE WILD

Corbett Tiger Reserve

(Uttarakhand, Northern India): Situated in the foothills of the Himalayas, Corbett is India's first national park and holds a unique allure. The real Corbett experience lies in Dhikala, the core of the forest, where rustic forest rest houses offer an immersive wildlife encounter. Staying in these cozy cottages amidst the sounds of trumpeting elephants and roaring tigers is a truly special experience. Corbett is also known for exceptional birdwatching, breathtaking landscapes, knowledgeable guides, and skilled drivers.

Kanha Tiger Reserve

(Madhya Pradesh, Central India): Known for its vast meadows, majestic Sal forests, and and an impressive array of wildlife. It is a paradise for serious wildlife enthusiasts, with a healthy tiger population and an abundance of other mammals and birds. The stunning landscapes make every corner of the park an ideal backdrop for wildlife photography. In addition to tigers, Kanha is home to the iconic hard ground Barasingha deer, wild dogs, sloth bears, leopards, and a variety of other wildlife species.
